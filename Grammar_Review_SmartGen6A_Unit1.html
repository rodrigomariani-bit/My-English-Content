1. Adverbs of Frequency

What are adverbs of frequency?

Adverbs of frequency tell us how often something happens. They range from 100% (all the time) to 0% (not at any time).

AdverbFrequencyExample
always100%I always brush my teeth before bed.
usually80%My mum usually drinks coffee for breakfast.
often70%My friends often go to school by bus.
sometimes50%We sometimes go to the cinema on weekends.
rarely20%Daddy rarely eats fast food.
never0%Hannah is never on time for school.

Lessons 2A & 2B

2. Word Order – Where Do They Go?

Rule 1: Before the main verb

Most adverbs of frequency go before the main verb (except the verb to be).

Lucas often walks to school.
We rarely eat dinner after 9 p.m.
I always brush my teeth before bed.

Rule 2: After the verb TO BE

When the main verb is be (am / is / are / was / were), the adverb comes after it.

Omar is never late for school.
They are usually very busy in the morning.

Rule 3: Sometimes & Usually – flexible position

These two adverbs can also go at the beginning or end of a sentence.

Sometimes, I forget my homework.
I forget my homework sometimes.
Usually, she drinks tea in the morning.
🧠 Quick tip: The position does NOT change in questions or negatives.
✅ Does Lucas often walk to school?
✅ Lucas doesn't often walk to school.

Lesson 2A

3. Special Rules for Never & Ever

Never = already negative

Never means "not at any time", so we do NOT use it with don't / doesn't / isn't. That would create a double negative.

I never drink coffee at night. ✅
I don't never drink coffee at night. ❌
💡 Instead of never + negative, we use ever + negative:
I don't ever drink coffee at night. ✅

Ever in questions

We don't usually use never in questions. We use ever instead.

Do you ever eat breakfast in bed?

Possible answers: No, never. / Sometimes. / I rarely do.

4. Adverbial Phrases of Frequency

What are they?

⚠️ Special case: hardly ever

Hardly ever means "almost never" and behaves like an adverb of frequency for word order:

I hardly ever eat fast food. (before the main verb)
They are hardly ever late for school. (after verb to be)

Lesson 4A

5. Position of Adverbial Phrases

Main position: end of the sentence

Most adverbial phrases of frequency go at the end of the sentence.

I drink tea every morning.
I take vitamin C once a day.
We have English class on Fridays.
My cousins visit me from time to time.

Alternative: beginning of the sentence (for emphasis)

For emphasis, we can put the phrase at the beginning. In this case, we add a comma.

Most of the time, we have lunch at home.
On Sundays, we go to a restaurant.
📌 Summary:
• Adverbs (always, usually, often, etc.) → before the verb / after be
• Phrases (every day, once a week, etc.) → end of sentence (or beginning + comma)
• hardly ever → follows adverb rules (before verb / after be)
