Warm‑up: small talk; set the promise: “By the end, you’ll feel ready.”

Where you’ll speak English

Airport Sections

Follow the path: Check‑in → Security → Immigration/Customs → Inside Airport → Boarding Gate

1) 🎫 Check‑in Counter

  • Where do I check in?
  • Agent: “May I see your passport and ticket?”
  • You: “Here’s my passport.”

2) 🔒 Security

  • Officer: “Remove your shoes, belt, and laptop.”
  • You: “Okay. Do I need to remove this?”

3) 🛂 Immigration / Customs

  • Officer: “What’s the purpose of your visit?”
  • You: “Tourism.” (keep it short)

4) 🚶 Inside Airport / ✈️ Boarding

  • Where is gate 23?
  • Gate: “Now boarding group 3.”
At the counter

🎫 Check‑in Counter

Listen first (YOU WILL HEAR), then repeat (YOU CAN SAY).

YOU WILL HEAR

  • “May I see your passport and ticket?”
  • “How many bags are you checking?”
  • “Did you pack your bags yourself?”
  • “Window or aisle seat?”

YOU CAN SAY

  • “Here’s my passport.”
  • “I have one checked bag.” / “Just carry‑on.”
  • “Yes, I packed them myself.”
  • “Window seat, please.” / “Aisle seat, please.”
Teacher tip

Run call‑and‑response drills. Repeat 2–3× for muscle memory.

Follow instructions

🔒 Security

Be ready to remove items and walk through the scanner.

YOU WILL HEAR

  • “Please remove your shoes, belt, and laptop.”
  • “Empty your pockets.”
  • “Step through the scanner.”
  • “Arms up.”

YOU CAN SAY

  • “Okay.” / “Sure.”
  • “Do I need to remove this?”
  • “Sorry, I don’t understand.”
  • “Like this?”
Short & simple answers

🛂 Immigration / Customs

Answer politely and briefly. No extra details.

THEY WILL ASK

  • “What’s the purpose of your visit?”
  • “How long will you stay?”
  • “Where will you be staying?”
  • “Is this your first time in the US?”

YOU SHOULD ANSWER

  • “Tourism.” / “Vacation.”
  • “Two weeks.” / “Ten days.”
  • “With family.” / “At a hotel.”
  • “Yes, it is.” / “No, I’ve been here before.”
Extra: “What will you do in the US?” → “Sightseeing.” / “Visit family.”
Teacher tip

Model bad vs. good answers; practice 3–4× with variations.

When you’re lost or confused

❓ Asking for Help

Use these anytime—people will help you!

Essential phrases

  • “Excuse me, where is gate ___?”
  • “Where can I find ___?”
  • “Can you help me, please?”
  • “I’m lost. Where is ___?”

Emergency phrase ⭐

“Sorry, I don’t understand. Can you speak more slowly, please?”

Repeat 3× with the student. Confidence boost guaranteed.

Understand the airport language

🔊 Signs & Announcements

Common words on boards & loudspeakers

On the boards

  • On time = no delay
  • Delayed = leaving later
  • Cancelled = flight won't go
  • Gate change = different gate
  • Final call = last chance to board

Over the speaker

  • “Now boarding groups 1 to 3.”
  • “Passenger [Your Name], please proceed to gate C12.”
  • “Security alert. Do not leave bags unattended.”
  • “We are experiencing a short delay.”

If you miss something → “Sorry, could you repeat that, please?”

Find places fast

🧭 Directions & Navigation

Ask clearly. Use short questions.

Ask

  • “Excuse me, where is gate C10?”
  • “Is it this way?” / “Straight ahead?”
  • “How long does it take to walk to gate C12?”
  • “Is there an elevator / escalator nearby?”

Understand replies

  • Go straight, then turn left/right.
  • Upstairs / downstairs
  • Next to / across from / behind
  • “It’s a 5‑minute walk.”
When things go wrong

⚠️ Problems & Solutions

Stay calm. Be polite. Use these scripts.

Lost baggage

  • “My bag didn’t arrive.”
  • “Where is the baggage service office?”
  • “Here’s my baggage claim tag.”
  • “Can you deliver it to my hotel?”

Missed / changed flight

  • “I missed my connection. What are my options?”
  • “Can you rebook me on the next flight?”
  • “Is there a standby list?”
  • “Can I change seats? Aisle, if possible.”
Gate issues: “My boarding pass isn’t scanning. Is there a problem? What should I do?”
Useful places inside

🧩 Services & Amenities

Ask for

  • “Where can I buy a SIM card?”
  • “Is there free Wi‑Fi?” / “What’s the password?”
  • “Where can I charge my phone?”
  • “Where is the restroom?”
  • “Is there a water fountain?”

Food & allergies

  • “Do you have vegetarian options?”
  • “Does this contain nuts?”
  • “Can I have a bottle of water, please?”
After you land

🚌 Ground Transportation

Ask

  • “Where is rideshare pickup (Uber/Lyft)?”
  • “How do I get to downtown?”
  • “Is there a shuttle bus to this hotel?”
  • “Where can I find a taxi?”

Money talk

  • “How much is the fare?”
  • “Do you accept credit card?”
  • “Can I pay cash?”
Cards, cash, exchange

💳 Money & Payments

Useful questions

  • “Do you accept contactless / tap to pay?”
  • “Is there an ATM nearby?”
  • “Where can I exchange money?”
  • “Are there any fees?”

Phrases

  • “Could I have a receipt, please?”
  • “The card didn’t go through. Can we try again?”
  • “I’ll pay by card / cash.”
Sound polite & keep the talk flowing

🗣️ Polite Language & Repair

Polite starters

  • “Excuse me…” / “Sorry to bother you…”
  • “Could you help me, please?”
  • “Would you mind repeating that?”

Repair & clarify

  • “How do you spell that?”
  • “Can you write it down, please?”
  • “One moment, let me check my documents.”
Understand gates & times

⏱️ Numbers, Time & Spelling

Time & gates

  • Boarding at 10:25 → “ten twenty‑five”
  • Gate C12 → “see twelve”
  • Row 17, Seat 17A

Spell your name

  • “My name is Rodrigo. That’s R‑O‑D‑R‑I‑G‑O.”
  • Ask: “Could you spell that for me, please?”
Say it clearly

🎧 Quick Pronunciation Help

Tricky words

  • aisle (corridor) → “ai‑uhl”
  • visa → “vee‑zuh”
  • schedule (US) → “ske‑jool”
  • receipt → “ri‑SEET” (p is silent)

Sound confident

  • Speak slowly and clearly.
  • Use please and thank you.
  • Make eye contact & smile 🙂
