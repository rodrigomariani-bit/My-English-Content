1 Likes & Dislikes

Expressing preferences using special expressions + gerund / noun

Key Expressions

ExpressionMeaningLike / Dislike?
really enjoy + -ing / nounlike a lot👍 Like
be (really) into + -ing / nounbe interested in👍 Like
be crazy about + -ing / nounlove intensely👍 Like
love + -ing / nounlike very much👍 Like
can't stand + -ing / nounhate👎 Dislike
be not interested in + -ing / noundon't care about👎 Dislike
be not into + -ing / noundon't like👎 Dislike

Grammar Rule

After all these expressions, use the -ing form of the verb or a noun. Never use the infinitive (to + verb).
✅ I really enjoy reading. (gerund)
✅ I'm crazy about chocolate. (noun)
✅ I can't stand cooking. (gerund)
❌ I really enjoy to read.

Responding: So / Neither / Really?

SituationStructureExample
Same (positive)So + am/do/can + I"I love pizza." → "So do I."
Same (negative)Neither / Nor + am/do/can + I"I can't stand jazz." → "Neither can I."
Different (from +)Really? I + don't / can't / 'm not"I love sushi." → "Really? I don't."
Different (from −)Really? I + do / can / am"I can't stand it." → "Really? I can."

2 Differences & Similarities

Comparing yourself to family members and friends

Expressions for Similarity (S)

He's just like his father.
My cousin looks exactly like / looks a lot like my aunt.
My sister takes after my mum.

Expressions for Difference (D)

I look nothing like my mother.
She's nothing like her father.

Useful Pocket Expressions

  • What I love/hate most about him/her is (that)…
  • He's/She's so… that…
  • That's why I (don't) like him/her.

4 Narrative Tenses: Past Simple & Past Continuous

Telling stories about past events

TenseUseForm
Past ContinuousSet the scene / background action (longer action)was / were + verb + -ing
Past SimpleShort completed action that interrupted the backgroundverb + -ed (regular) or irregular form

Key Connectors

Charlotte was sitting on the couch when an intruder broke into her house.
Brutus attacked the intruder while he was speaking to the victim.
when → usually followed by Past Simple (the interruption).
while → usually followed by Past Continuous (the ongoing action).

Pronunciation of -ed endings

/d//t//ɪd/
arrived, called, playedattacked, stopped, watchedwanted, needed, waited
The -ed is only pronounced as a separate syllable (/ɪd/) when the verb ends in t or d.

5 Reporting What People Said

Using reporting verbs + object + (not) to + infinitive

Structure

Subject + reporting verb + object + (not) to + infinitive
FunctionVerbExample
AdviceadvisedKate advised Lily to forget Chris.
RequestaskedChris asked Lily to let him explain.
EncouragementencouragedShe encouraged Lily to accept.
InvitationinvitedJake invited her to see a film.
ReminderremindedHe reminded Lily to text Jake.
CommandtoldShe told him not to call her again.

Negative Form

not comes BEFORE to:
✅ She told me not to be upset.
❌ She told me to not be upset.

Pronunciation: Weak Forms

In connected speech, object pronouns (him, her, them, us) are pronounced with a weak /ə/ sound: "told him" → /toʊld ɪm/

6 Modal Verbs: must, should, may

Obligation, advice, and possibility

ModalUseExample
mustObligation / something importantI must be careful with personal info.
shouldAdvice / recommendationTeens should limit their time online.
mayPossibilitySocial networks may promote community.
All three modals are followed by the base form of the verb (no to, no -s, no -ing).
✅ You should study more.
❌ You should to study more.
❌ You should studying more.

7 Present Perfect & Past Simple

Talking about life experiences and their details

TenseUseForm
Present PerfectIntroduce a past experience (no specific time)have / has + past participle
Past SimpleGive details about a specific timeverb + -ed / irregular
"I've eaten very odd things quite a few times." (experience → Pres. Perfect)
"It was when I was in Cambodia." (detail → Past Simple)

"Have you ever eaten in a totally unique place?"
"Well, I once went to a restaurant in Germany."

Answering "Have you ever…?" Questions

AnswerExamples
Yes (Present Perfect)Yes, I've tried it twice / loads of times / a couple of times.
Yes (Past Simple)Yes, I tried it just the other day / some months ago / when I was on holidays.
NoNo, never, but I'd like to / but I hope to one day / and I wouldn't like to.
