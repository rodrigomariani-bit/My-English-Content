Subject Pronouns

Used as the subject (doer) of the verb

📌 What is a Subject Pronoun?
A subject pronoun replaces the noun that performs the action of the verb. It always comes before the verb in a sentence.

Structure: Subject Pronoun + Verb + …
Example: "She loves coffee."
Person Pronoun Example sentence Portuguese
1st SingularII like music.Eu
2nd SingularyouYou are smart.Você / Tu
3rd Singular ♂heHe plays soccer.Ele
3rd Singular ♀sheShe is a teacher.Ela
3rd Singular (thing)itIt is raining.Ele/Ela (coisa)
1st PluralweWe go to school.Nós
2nd PluralyouYou are all welcome.Vocês
3rd PluraltheyThey live in São Paulo.Eles / Elas

Examples

  • I am studying English every day. Eu estudo inglês todos os dias.
  • She is my best friend. Ela é minha melhor amiga.
  • They are going to the party tonight. Eles vão à festa esta noite.
  • It is a beautiful day! É um dia lindo!
💡
Attention! In English, "I" is always capitalized, even in the middle of a sentence. And "you" is the same for singular and plural — context tells you which one!

Object Pronouns

Used as the object (receiver) of the action

📌 What is an Object Pronoun?
An object pronoun replaces a noun that receives the action of the verb, or follows a preposition.

Structure: Subject + Verb + Object Pronoun
Example: "She called him." | "This is for you."
Person Pronoun Example sentence Portuguese
1st SingularmeShe called me.Me / Mim
2nd SingularyouI can help you.Te / Você
3rd Singular ♂himWe invited him.O / Ele
3rd Singular ♀herI know her.A / Ela
3rd Singular (thing)itI found it!O / A
1st PluralusHe told us the news.Nos / Nós
2nd PluralyouI'll meet you all there.Vocês
3rd PluralthemCan you see them?Os / As / Eles

Examples

  • My teacher helps me with my homework. Minha professora me ajuda com o dever de casa.
  • I gave him a birthday present. Eu dei a ele um presente de aniversário.
  • This book is for her. Este livro é para ela.
  • We waited for them a long time. Nós esperamos por eles muito tempo.
💡
Key rule: Object pronouns come after the verb or after a preposition (for, with, to, about, etc.).
✅ "Talk to me."  |  ✅ "She loves him."  |  ❌ "Me go." (wrong — needs subject pronoun: "I go.")

Possessive Adjectives

Show ownership — always followed by a noun

📌 What is a Possessive Adjective?
A possessive adjective shows that something belongs to someone. It always comes before a noun and describes it.

Structure: Possessive Adjective + Noun
Example: "This is my book." → the word my describes the noun book.
Person Possessive Adj. Example sentence Portuguese
1st SingularmyThis is my pen.Meu / Minha
2nd SingularyourIs this your phone?Seu / Sua (seu/sua)
3rd Singular ♂hisHis car is red.Dele
3rd Singular ♀herHer dress is blue.Dela
3rd Singular (thing)itsThe dog wags its tail.Dele/Dela (neutro)
1st PluralourThis is our classroom.Nosso / Nossa
2nd PluralyourYour books are here.De vocês
3rd PluraltheirTheir house is big.Deles / Delas

Examples

  • My mother is a nurse. Minha mãe é enfermeira.
  • His favorite color is green. A cor favorita dele é verde.
  • We love our school. Nós amamos nossa escola.
  • The cat hurt its paw. O gato machucou a pata.
⚠️
Common mistake: Don't confuse its (possessive) with it's (= it is).
✅ "The cat licks its fur."  |  ✅ "It's a beautiful cat." (= It is a beautiful cat.)

Possessive Pronouns

Stand alone — replace the noun entirely

📌 What is a Possessive Pronoun?
A possessive pronoun shows ownership AND replaces the noun — so the noun is not repeated.

Structure: Possessive Pronoun (alone — no noun after it!)
Example: "This book is mine." → mine = my book
Person Possessive Pron. Example sentence Portuguese
1st SingularmineThis bag is mine.O meu / A minha
2nd SingularyoursThat seat is yours.O seu / A sua
3rd Singular ♂hisThe red bike is his.O dele
3rd Singular ♀hersThat jacket is hers.O dela / A dela
3rd Singular (thing)its(rarely used alone)—
1st PluraloursThis project is ours.O nosso / A nossa
2nd PluralyoursThe victory is yours.De vocês
3rd PluraltheirsThis house is theirs.Deles / Delas

Possessive Adj. vs Possessive Pronoun

  • This is my book.  →  (Possessive Adjective — my + noun "book") Este é o meu livro.
  • This book is mine.  →  (Possessive Pronoun — stands alone, no noun after) Este livro é meu.
  • Is that your umbrella?  →  (Possessive Adjective) Aquele é o seu guarda-chuva?
  • No, that umbrella is yours!  →  (Possessive Pronoun) Não, aquele guarda-chuva é seu!
💡
Simple trick: If you can replace the word with "it belongs to me/you/him…", use a Possessive Pronoun. If the word is followed by a noun, use a Possessive Adjective.

Reflexive Pronouns

When the subject and object are the same person

📌 What is a Reflexive Pronoun?
A reflexive pronoun is used when the subject and the object of the sentence are the same person or thing. It ends in -self (singular) or -selves (plural).

Two main uses:
1. Reflexive action — the subject does something to themselves: "She hurt herself."
2. Emphatic use — to stress that someone did something alone: "I made this cake myself!"
Person Reflexive Pronoun Example sentence Portuguese
1st SingularmyselfI hurt myself.Eu mesmo / me
2nd SingularyourselfBe yourself.Você mesmo / se
3rd Singular ♂himselfHe cooked himself dinner.Ele mesmo / se
3rd Singular ♀herselfShe taught herself guitar.Ela mesma / se
3rd Singular (thing)itselfThe door closed itself.Em si mesmo
1st PluralourselvesWe enjoyed ourselves.Nós mesmos / nos
2nd PluralyourselvesHelp yourselves!Vocês mesmos
3rd PluralthemselvesThey introduced themselves.Eles mesmos / se

Uses of Reflexive Pronouns

  • 🔄 Reflexive: He cut himself while cooking. Ele se cortou enquanto cozinhava.
  • 🔄 Reflexive: She looked at herself in the mirror. Ela se olhou no espelho.
  • 💪 Emphatic: I built this table myself! Eu mesmo construí esta mesa!
  • 💪 Emphatic: The president himself signed the document. O próprio presidente assinou o documento.
  • 🎉 Common phrase: We really enjoyed ourselves at the party! Nós nos divertimos muito na festa!
💡
How to form reflexive pronouns:
• Singular: my/your/him/her/it + -self → myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself
• Plural: our/your/them + -selves → ourselves, yourselves, themselves

WH Question Words

Words used to ask for specific information

📌 What are WH Question Words?
WH question words (also called interrogative words) are used at the beginning of a question to ask for specific information. They are called "WH words" because most of them start with the letters W-H.

Structure: WH word + auxiliary verb + subject + main verb…?
Example: "Where do you live?"
WHO
Quem
Used for
Asking about people — the identity of a person.
Who is your best friend?
→ Maria is my best friend.
Who called you last night?
→ My sister called me.
WHAT
O que / Qual
Used for
Asking about things, actions, or ideas.
What is your name?
→ My name is Lucas.
What do you like to eat?
→ I like pizza.
WHERE
Onde
Used for
Asking about a place or location.
Where do you live?
→ I live in Colatina.
Where is the bathroom?
→ It's on the second floor.
WHEN
Quando
Used for
Asking about time, dates, or moments.
When is your birthday?
→ It's in March.
When did you wake up?
→ I woke up at 7 am.
WHY
Por que
Used for
Asking for a reason or cause. Often answered with "Because…"
Why are you late?
→ Because there was traffic.
Why do you study English?
→ Because I want to travel.
HOW
Como
Used for
Asking about manner, condition, or degree. Has many combinations!
How are you?
→ I'm fine, thank you!
How do you go to school?
→ I go by bus.
WHICH
Qual / Quais
Used for
Asking to choose between specific options (limited choices).
Which color do you prefer?
→ I prefer blue.
Which team won the game?
→ Brazil won!
WHOSE
De quem
Used for
Asking about possession — who something belongs to.
Whose bag is this?
→ It's Ana's bag.
Whose turn is it?
→ It's your turn!

⚡ HOW + Adjective/Adverb — Common Combinations

  • How old are you? → I'm 15 years old. Quantos anos você tem?
  • How much does it cost? → It costs R$20. Quanto custa? (incontável)
  • How many students are in the class? → There are 30. Quantos...? (contável)
  • How far is the school? → It's 2 km away. Qual a distância?
  • How long does the trip take? → About 3 hours. Quanto tempo dura?
  • How often do you exercise? → Three times a week. Com que frequência?
💡
WHAT vs WHICH?
Use WHAT when the options are unlimited: "What sport do you play?"
Use WHICH when choosing from a specific/limited set: "Which sport do you prefer — soccer or basketball?"
